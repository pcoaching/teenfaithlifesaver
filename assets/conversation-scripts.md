# 47 CONVERSATION SCRIPTS
## Word-for-Word Language for Every Situation

## SCRIPTS 1-10: THE BASICS

### Script 1: Starting the Ownership Conversation
**When:** Age 10-12, before confirmation  
**Goal:** Move from "we believe" to "I believe"

> "You're getting older, and I want to talk about something important. You've been Catholic your whole life because that's what our family is. But I want you to know that at some point, this faith needs to become yours—not just inherited. Not yet. But I want us to start talking about what YOU believe, not just what we do together. What questions do you have about God that you've never asked me?"

### Script 2: When They Ask About Doubt
**When:** They express uncertainty  
**Goal:** Normalize without minimizing

> "I'm glad you told me. Doubt isn't the enemy of faith—it's part of it. Every saint had doubts. The question isn't whether you doubt, but what you do with it. Let's explore it together. What specifically feels uncertain to you?"

### Script 3: The Mission Conversation Opener
**When:** Age 12-14  
**Goal:** Connect faith to purpose

> "I want to tell you something I don't think I've said clearly. God made you for a reason. Not just to be a good person or follow rules. There's something specific He wants you to do in this world. Have you ever thought about what that might be?"

### Script 4: When They Roll Their Eyes at Prayer
**When:** Disrespect during family prayer  
**Goal:** Address attitude without power struggle

> "I see you're frustrated. Can we talk about what's really bothering you? Is it the prayer itself, or something else? I need you to be respectful, but I also want to understand."

### Script 5: When They Ask Why We Go to Mass
**When:** Resistance to attending  
**Goal:** Explain meaning, not just obligation

> "That's a fair question. We don't go because we have to. We go because that's where we meet Jesus—in the Eucharist. Everything else is secondary. If you don't believe that, we need to talk about why. But that's the core of it."

### Script 6: When They Mention a Friend Left the Church
**When:** They bring up peer departure  
**Goal:** Explore without interrogating

> "I'm sorry to hear that. How did your friend seem when they talked about it? [Listen] What did you think about what they said?"

### Script 7: When They Ask About Another Religion
**When:** Curiosity about other faiths  
**Goal:** Inform without fear

> "It's good to know what others believe. What specifically are you curious about? I can explain what makes Catholicism different, but I also want you to understand why we believe what we believe."

### Script 8: When They Question a Church Teaching
**When:** They disagree with doctrine  
**Goal:** Create dialogue, not debate

> "That's a serious question, and I respect that you're thinking about it. I may not have all the answers, but I can tell you why the Church teaches what it does. Can we look at it together? And if I don't know, we'll find out."

### Script 9: When They Say Religion Is "Private"
**When:** They want to avoid discussion  
**Goal:** Respect boundaries while staying connected

> "I understand wanting privacy. But faith isn't just private—it's who we are. I'm not going to force you to talk, but I am going to keep inviting you to conversation. I'm always here when you're ready."

### Script 10: When They Ask if You Ever Doubt
**When:** They want to know you're human  
**Goal:** Model honest faith

> "Yes. I've had doubts. I still do sometimes. Faith isn't the absence of doubt—it's choosing to believe despite it. Want me to tell you about a time I doubted and what happened?"

## SCRIPTS 11-20: APOLOGETICS AND HARD QUESTIONS

### Script 11: Why Does God Allow Suffering?
> "That's the hardest question there is. I don't have a complete answer. But I know this: God didn't create suffering—human choice did. And God entered into our suffering in Jesus. He didn't stay distant. He suffered with us and for us. That doesn't make suffering okay, but it means we're not alone in it."

### Script 12: What About Science and Evolution?
> "The Church doesn't oppose science. Evolution describes how life developed; it doesn't explain why we're here or who created the process. Many great scientists were Catholic. Faith and science answer different questions."

### Script 13: Why Is the Church Against [LGBTQ/Abortion/etc]?
> "The Church teaches that certain actions don't lead to human flourishing, even if they feel right in the moment. That doesn't mean we judge people—we're all sinners. But we believe truth exists, and it's not determined by feelings or culture. Want to look at what the Church actually teaches?"

### Script 14: How Do You Know God Exists?
> "I can't prove it like a math equation. But I see evidence: the universe had a beginning, life is too complex for chance, conscience points to moral law, and millions of people have encountered God personally. Plus, I've experienced Him myself. That's not proof, but it's reason enough to believe."

### Script 15: Why Catholic and Not Just Christian?
> "All Christians share core beliefs about Jesus. Catholics believe He gave us the Eucharist—His real presence—and established a Church with authority to teach. That's the difference. Other Christians are our brothers and sisters, but we believe the fullness of faith is in the Catholic Church."

### Script 16: The Bible Is Full of Contradictions
> "Some things seem contradictory because we're reading ancient texts with modern eyes. Scholars have addressed most apparent contradictions. But even if something remains unclear, that doesn't disprove the whole. The core message is consistent: God loves us, we sinned, Jesus saves us."

### Script 17: What About Other Religions?
> "Other religions have truth in them—Catholics believe that. But we believe Jesus is the fullness of truth. Other religions are like different paths up the mountain; Christianity claims Jesus IS the mountain. That's either true or it's not."

### Script 18: Why Do Bad Things Happen to Good People?
> "Because we live in a broken world. Bad things happen to everyone—good, bad, religious, not. The question isn't why bad things happen, but how we respond. Faith doesn't prevent suffering; it gives us strength to endure it and hope beyond it."

### Script 19: I Don't Feel Anything When I Pray
> "Feelings aren't the measure of prayer. Sometimes prayer feels dry—that's normal. The saints called it 'dark nights.' What matters is showing up, not feeling something. Keep praying. The feelings come and go; the relationship remains."

### Script 20: How Can a Loving God Send People to Hell?
> "God doesn't send people to hell—people choose it. Hell is the state of being separated from God by our own choice. God offers everyone grace and mercy. Those in hell are there because they rejected God, not because God rejected them."

## SCRIPTS 21-30: CRISIS SITUATIONS

### Script 21: When They Refuse to Go to Mass
> "I hear you don't want to go. I need you to understand: this isn't optional for our family. But I also want to understand why. Can you tell me what's really going on? [Listen] Okay. You still need to come, but let's figure out how to make this better."

### Script 22: When They Announce They Don't Believe Anymore
> "Thank you for telling me. I know that took courage. I'm not going to panic or argue. I want to understand. When you say you don't believe, what specifically don't you believe? God exists? Jesus is God? The Church has authority? Help me understand where you are."

### Script 23: When They Say They Hate the Church
> "That's strong language. What specifically do you hate? The people? The rules? The history? Something that happened? I need to understand if this is about the Church or something else."

### Script 24: When They're Influenced by a Friend
> "I notice you've been spending a lot of time with [name]. How do you feel about the way they talk about faith? [Listen] Friends influence us—that's normal. I just want you to be aware of who's shaping your thinking."

### Script 25: When They Discover Something Scandalous
> "I heard about that too. It's terrible. People in the Church—including leaders—have done terrible things. That doesn't mean the faith is false; it means people are sinful. Even priests and bishops. The Church is made of sinners being saved by Jesus."

### Script 26: When They Want to Stop Confirmation
> "Confirmation isn't just a ceremony—it's receiving the Holy Spirit's power in a new way. I understand if you're not feeling it right now, but I'd ask you to go through with it anyway. You can receive the grace even if you don't feel it. And we can talk about your doubts."

### Script 27: When They Reject Moral Teaching
> "I know what the culture says about this. But I need you to understand: morality isn't determined by popularity polls. The Church teaches what leads to human flourishing, even when it's hard. Can we look at why the Church teaches this?"

### Script 28: When They Want to Date a Non-Catholic
> "Who you date matters—a lot. It's not about their religion being 'wrong.' It's about sharing the most important thing in your life. If faith is central to you, dating someone who doesn't share it creates a fundamental divide. Let's talk about what you're looking for in a relationship."

### Script 29: When They're Experimenting with Drugs/Alcohol
> "We need to talk about what I found. [State facts] This isn't about punishment. It's about your safety and your future. But more than that—it's about who you're becoming. Let's talk about what's really going on."

### Script 30: When They're Cutting/Self-Harm
> "I saw the marks. I'm not angry. I'm scared, and I love you. This is serious, and we need to get you help. This isn't something we handle alone. We're going to talk to someone who knows how to help. You're not in trouble—you're loved."

## SCRIPTS 31-40: COLLEGE AND INDEPENDENCE

### Script 31: Preparing for College Secularism
> "College will challenge everything you believe—not just intellectually, but socially. You'll be surrounded by people who think faith is stupid. I need you to be ready. Let's go through the Faith Stress Test before you leave."

### Script 32: Setting Boundaries Before College
> "You're an adult now, or close to it. But while you're under our roof and we're supporting you, we have expectations. Let's be clear about what those are—and what happens if they're not met."

### Script 33: When They Want to Go to a Secular College
> "I understand the appeal. But I need you to know what you're walking into. Secular universities are hostile to faith—not just neutral. If you go, you need to be prepared. Let's talk about what that preparation looks like."

### Script 34: The College Goodbye Conversation
> "I'm going to miss you. More than that, I'm worried about you. Not because I don't trust you—because I know what's coming. Promise me you'll stay connected. Promise me you'll find a Catholic community there. Promise me you'll call when it gets hard."

### Script 35: When They Stop Going to Mass at College
> "I noticed you didn't go to Mass last Sunday. [Pause] What's going on? [Listen] I can't make you go. But I can tell you that skipping Mass is usually the first step away from faith. Is that what you want?"

### Script 36: When They Change Their Major to Something Concerning
> "I support your education. But I need to understand how this major fits with who you are and what you believe. Some fields are more hostile to faith than others. Let's talk about how you'll navigate that."

### Script 37: When They Bring Home a Partner You Disapprove Of
> "I want to welcome [name]. But I also need to be honest: I'm concerned about [specific issue]. I hope I'm wrong. But I'm going to be watching, and I'm going to be praying. And I'm always here to talk."

### Script 38: When They Announce They're Moving In With Someone
> "I can't stop you. You're an adult. But I need you to know how serious this is. Living together isn't just 'practicing marriage'—it makes marriage less likely to succeed. And it's not compatible with what we believe. I love you, but I can't pretend I'm okay with this."

### Script 39: When They Get Engaged Too Young
> "I want you to be happy. But I also want you to succeed. Getting married at [age] is statistically risky. That doesn't mean it can't work. But it means you need to be extra intentional. Let's talk about what marriage actually requires."

### Script 40: When They Announce They're Pregnant
> "[Pause] I love you. No matter what. Let's talk about what you need and how we can help. This isn't the end of your life—it's a hard beginning. But we're in this together."

## SCRIPTS 41-47: RECONNECTION AND HEALING

### Script 41: When You Need to Apologize
> "I need to apologize. I've been [specific behavior]. That was wrong. I'm sorry. I'm working on it. Will you forgive me?"

### Script 42: When You Want to Rebuild Connection
> "I feel like we've drifted apart. I miss talking to you. Can we start over? Just spend some time together—no agenda, no lectures?"

### Script 43: When They're Open to Talking Again
> "Thank you for being willing to talk. I'm not going to waste this. I want to understand you better. Tell me about what's really going on in your life."

### Script 44: When They Ask Why You Care So Much
> "Because I love you. Because I believe faith is the most important thing in life. Because I've seen what happens when people lose it. And because I couldn't live with myself if I didn't fight for your soul."

### Script 45: When They Accuse You of Being Controlling
> "I hear you. I don't want to control you—I want to guide you. But maybe I've been too heavy-handed. Let's talk about what appropriate boundaries look like at your age."

### Script 46: When They Come Back to Faith
> "I'm so glad you told me. I've been praying for this. Welcome home. Let's take it slow—no pressure, no performance. Just come back to Jesus."

### Script 47: When Nothing Is Working
> "I don't know what else to do. I've tried everything I know. But I'm not giving up on you. I never will. Even when you push me away. Even when you hate me. I'm your parent, and I love you. That doesn't change."
