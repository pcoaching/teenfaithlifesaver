The Retention Mindset

Why youth group isn't enough and the 3 conversations you must have before age 13.

The Problem with "Youth Group"

Most Catholic parents outsource their teen's faith formation to youth group. Here's the uncomfortable truth: youth group is designed for kids who are already engaged. It's not a retention strategy.

By the time your teen is rolling their eyes at youth group, the battle is already half lost. The parents who win start earlier and build something different.

The 3 Conversations Before Age 13

There are three conversations every Catholic parent must have with their child before they turn 13. Miss these, and you're playing defense for the next decade.

  • Conversation 1: The Ownership Conversation. Moving faith from "what we do" to "what I believe." This must happen before peer pressure peaks.
  • Conversation 2: The Doubt Conversation. Normalizing questions before they become crises. Every teen will doubt. Will they doubt with you or against you?
  • Conversation 3: The Mission Conversation. Connecting faith to purpose. Teens leave when faith feels like rules without meaning.

Becoming the Trusted Source

Your teen will get answers to their hard questions. The only question is: from whom? If you're not their trusted source, TikTok will be. Their friends will be. The culture will be.

This module shows you exactly how to become the person they come to first — even when the questions scare you.

Key Takeaways

  • Youth group supplements; it doesn't replace parent-led formation
  • The 3 conversations must happen before age 13
  • Your goal is to become their first call for hard questions
  • Retention starts with relationship, not information
  • The mindset shift: from managing behavior to forming disciples
