When Everything Feels Broken

Your teen refuses to go to Mass. They announce they don't believe anymore. They roll their eyes at everything you say about faith. The relationship feels shattered. This is the moment that determines everything.

Most parents panic. They argue harder. They quote scripture. They enforce rules more strictly. And they push their teen further away. There's a better way.

The Exact Words That Rebuild Connection

This module gives you scripts—actual words to say—for the most painful moments:

  • When they refuse Mass: The response that respects their freedom while maintaining boundaries
  • When they announce they don't believe: How to respond with curiosity instead of panic
  • When they question Church teaching: The difference between honest questioning and rebellion
  • When they shut down completely: How to stay connected when they won't talk
  • When they make choices that scare you: Balancing concern with control

When to Get Outside Help

Some situations require professional intervention. This module shows you the red lines—when to call a priest, a counselor, or a crisis intervention service. And when to keep fighting for the relationship yourself.

Key Takeaways

  • Panic pushes teens away; strategic response invites them back
  • Specific scripts for the most painful moments
  • Curiosity beats confrontation when faith is questioned
  • Connection must be maintained even when agreement isn't possible
  • Know the red lines that require professional intervention
