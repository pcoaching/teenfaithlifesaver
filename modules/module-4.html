The College Transition

Preparing teens for the intellectual assault of secular university.

The Freshman Assault

Secular universities don't just challenge faith—they dismantle it systematically. Freshman year hits like a hurricane: philosophy classes that "prove" God doesn't exist, sociology professors who mock religion as ignorance, dorm cultures that treat chastity as pathology.

The teens who survive aren't smarter. They're prepared. This module is that preparation.

The Faith Stress Test

Every 17-year-old should pass a faith stress test before leaving home. This isn't about memorizing answers—it's about knowing their own faith well enough to withstand pressure. The test covers:

  • Intellectual pressure: Can they articulate why they believe when professors challenge them?
  • Social pressure: Can they maintain boundaries when everyone around them doesn't?
  • Moral pressure: Do they know where their lines are before they're tested?
  • Emotional pressure: Can they handle loneliness, failure, and doubt without abandoning faith?

Staying Connected Without Hovering

The parents who lose their kids in college often make one of two mistakes: they hover (driving their teen away) or they disappear (leaving them isolated). This module shows you the third way—strategic connection that supports without smothering.

Key Takeaways

  • Secular universities systematically dismantle faith—preparation is essential
  • The faith stress test identifies gaps before they become crises
  • Intellectual, social, moral, and emotional pressure each require different preparation
  • Strategic connection beats hovering or disappearing
  • The goal is a faith that survives independence
